# Gardn World (marketing) — Ambition

## One-line pitch

Gardn World is the marketing and content surface that sits alongside the Gardn app — driving adoption, building the brand, and giving the gardening community a place to find Gardn before they download it. The job is conversion, not product delivery.

## The ambition

Gardn World succeeds when Gardn the app does. Its specific success criteria — traffic, conversion to install, brand awareness in the gardening space — are subordinate to the £1m+ ARR target on the app side. Gardn World should be the most useful gardening content destination on the open web and the most efficient acquisition channel for the app.

Operates on a different cadence from `gardn-native`: copy, SEO, content production, and conversion optimisation rather than sprints and releases. Same brand, different work.

## Claude's role

Three hats, all marketing-shaped.

1. **Content generation at scale** — guides, seasonal pieces, plant care articles, the long tail of SEO-aligned content that drives organic traffic.
2. **Thinking partner** for positioning, brand voice, and acquisition strategy.
3. **Build and deploy support** in Claude Code for the marketing site itself.

## Strategic bet

Consumer apps live or die on acquisition cost. A high-quality marketing surface that ranks organically for gardening queries — and that converts directly to app installs — is the single most leveraged thing the marketing side of Gardn can do. AI-assisted content production lets a two-person team produce volume that would normally require a content agency.

## What would kill it

The marketing site never reaches escape velocity on traffic, leaving Gardn dependent on paid acquisition with consumer-app unit economics. Or — quieter and more dangerous — the brand voice on `gardn-world` drifts from the brand voice in the app, fragmenting the product story across the two surfaces.
