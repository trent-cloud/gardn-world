How it works

Set it up once. It grows with you after that.

Draw your garden, let Border Scan name what’s in it, and everything that follows — the weekly plan, the alerts, the advice — is shaped by your spaces, your soil and your weather. Not a generic gardening app pointed at you.

Gardn is organised around four things your garden needs: Care · Intelligence · Nature · Community.

1

Trace your garden.

Draw your borders, beds and pots once — a simple 2D plan everything anchors to.

2

Name your plants.

Walk the beds with Border Scan. Each plant lands in its place, with a name and a rhythm.

3

Let it tend.

From there, the weekly plan, alerts and advice are shaped by your actual garden.

Border Scan

Fill your whole garden in one slow pan.

Hold up your phone and walk the border. Gardn checks each plant as you go — cross-referencing two sources — and drops a likely match into place, with a confidence level. No typing required.

Correct one if it's off and it remembers. The scan assesses each plant in context; you confirm what lands. Free to try each month, then part of your premium allowance.

Ask Gardn

The gardener’s answer, without the gardener.

Ask it the way you’d ask a neighbour who’d walked your borders — name this, why’s that struggling, what now. The reply comes back about your garden, not gardening in general.

Free to ask each month, with a wider allowance on premium. Turning a description into a picture is Inspired by Gardn — part of premium.

Health check

See what’s likely wrong, and what to do tonight.

A struggling plant or a tired patch of lawn — one photo, an honest assessment of what it’s likely seeing, and the next move. Calm about it, never catastrophising.

Gardn names what it thinks is happening and why, then gives you a short plan. You decide whether to act. Plants and lawn use the same simple flow. Free to check each month, then part of your premium allowance.

For nature

See how much your garden gives back.

A single score for how well your garden supports wildlife — and a short, doable list of what to add to lift it.

Set a goal or two and Gardn keeps an eye on them quietly. A garden is a habitat; this is how you watch it become one. Free.

Bird Listen

Name the birds singing in your garden.

Hold up your phone and Gardn listens, then suggests the most likely matches — with a confidence level for each. You confirm what you hear. No need to spot them.

Location-aware, across roughly 6,300 species worldwide, and free to use. Like Merlin for your own garden — honest about what it's sure of, and what it isn't. Every sighting logs to your garden's nature record.
